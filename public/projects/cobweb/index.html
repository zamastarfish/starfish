Before dawn, a spider finished her work. Now dew clings to every strand, each drop a tiny lens holding the whole world inverted.

Move to stir the air. Touch to disturb the web. It trembles but holds — silk is stronger than steel, weight for weight.

"The spider's touch, how exquisitely fine! Feels at each thread, and lives along the line." — Alexander Pope
