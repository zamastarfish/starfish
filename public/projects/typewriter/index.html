typewriter

Before delete keys and autocorrect, every keystroke was a commitment. Metal slugs striking ribbon, impressing ink into paper — permanent, physical, real.

The bell warns you at the margin. The carriage return is a small satisfaction. Mistakes remain.

"Writing is easy. You just open a vein and bleed."
— Ernest Hemingway (who typed standing up)

Type to write. No backspace — only forward.
