Turing

Concept

Reaction-diffusion systems, first described by Alan Turing in 1952. Two chemicals interact and diffuse at different rates, creating patterns that emerge spontaneously — spots, stripes, labyrinths.

In Nature

This is how zebras get stripes, leopards get spots, and coral forms its patterns. The math of biology, made visible.
